Some students felt very differently about the University’s COVID-19 policies, however: 5.8 percent of respondents wrote that the University’s COVID-19 policies are too strict.

These quotes have been edited for length and clarity.

“Considering virtually every student is vaccinated or will be vaccinated shortly after the semester begins, it is completely unacceptable for the school to have COVID-19 policies like mandating mask use for everyone indoors, restricting guests to dorms, and limiting the use of Dodge Fitness Center. I would like to ask whoever is willing to answer what Columbia perceives to be the point where they cease to do these things, given that COVID-19 is not looking like something that will be completely eradicated any time soon. Particularly with guest policy in dorms and availability in Dodge, I was sold these two as guarantees when deciding on a college, and so was everyone else. Restricting them is a massive disservice and frankly extremely disrespectful to the student body.”

“I am concerned socially, not for fear of catching COVID-19, but because the majority of clubs I participate in are performance- or conference-based. Because of Columbia’s new capacity restrictions on extracurricular and social gatherings, I am unsure how to stay involved. As a senior, I fear missing out on my last year of college life. I may never have another chance to do in-person theater (one of my passions) or simply go to a large party with college friends. It frustrates me even more because Columbia’s population is almost entirely vaccinated, making the restrictions feel pointless. I can go see a Broadway show at next-to-full capacity (or even an ‘academic event’ at Columbia), but clubs can’t have meetings with more than 25 people? It feels ridiculous.”

“As a New York City resident, I know how bad the pandemic can get. My family and I stayed in the city throughout the very worst of it in March, April, and May of 2020. I remember the refrigerator trucks parked alongside my local hospital, the fact that sirens eventually became white noise. That being said, I wish the University loosened up on its restrictions.
